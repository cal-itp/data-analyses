SacRt : The SacRT stop-level ridership dataset contains three types of records: weekday, daily, and other. Weekday rows have the Saturday and Sunday flags set to 0 and represent average boardings on Monday through Friday only. Daily rows have all day-of-week flags set to 1, meaning the stop operates every day; for these stops, the average_boardings reflects the average across all 7 days, calculated as an average over a three-month period. Importantly, in the daily type, Saturday and Sunday always appear together, either the stop runs both weekend days or neither, so there are no cases where only one weekend day is active. Other rows are exceptions, for example stops that don’t operate on a specific weekday, and need to be handled individually. There is just one case of Other where one of the stop shows 0 on a Tuesday.  I’m thinking that, for modeling purposes, since weekend-specific averages aren’t directly provided, the best approach would be to take stops with daily records that operate on weekends and use any available weekday data to estimate total weekend ridership using S + U = 7 × daily − 5 × weekday.
 Then, using GTFS data, we can allocate that weekend total across Saturday and Sunday based on the number of trips serving each stop. For example, if a stop has 60 trips on Saturday and 40 trips on Sunday, we would assign 60% of the weekend ridership to Saturday and 40% to Sunday.
